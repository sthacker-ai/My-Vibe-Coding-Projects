# Startup

## Overview
This topic explores the philosophy of continuous building and scaling limits, using the concept of "Antigravity" to overcome constraints in the startup environment. It emphasizes the necessity of persistent action and pushing boundaries rather than stopping due to perceived limitations.

## Core Ideas
- **Antigravity Limits:** Represents the current boundaries or constraints of a system or project.
- **Scaling Quotas:** Specific restrictions placed on growth, often measured on a weekly basis.
- **Continuous Building:** The principle of persistent development despite facing limitations.
- **Don't Stop Building:** The core philosophy emphasizing action and momentum in growth.
- Overcoming constraints requires pushing boundaries rather than accepting limits.

## What I Have Learned
The philosophy of continuous building suggests that startups must focus on momentum and persistent development even when facing scaling constraints. By viewing limits (Antigravity Limits) not as stopping points but as boundaries to be pushed, one can maintain continuous growth. The core lesson is that momentum is generated through persistent action, encouraging founders to focus on building despite temporary limitations.
