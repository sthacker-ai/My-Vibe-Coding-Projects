# Scaling Limits and Continuous Building with Antigravity

## Overview
This course explores the philosophy of continuous building and scaling limits, using the concept of "Antigravity" as an analogy for overcoming constraints in a startup environment. It emphasizes the importance of pushing boundaries rather than stopping due to perceived limitations.

## Key Concepts
- Antigravity Limits: Represents the current boundaries or constraints of a system or project.
- Scaling Quotas: The specific restrictions placed on growth, often measured weekly.
- Continuous Building: The principle of persistent development despite facing limitations.
- Don't Stop Building: The core philosophy emphasizing action and momentum.

## Chapter 1: Understanding Scaling Limits
The concept of "Antigravity limits" refers to the current boundaries or constraints imposed on a system. These limits define the current capacity or potential of the project. Recognizing these limits is the first step in understanding where growth currently resides.

## Chapter 2: The Power of Weekly Quotas
When limits are established, they are often implemented through measurable constraints like "weekly quotas." These quotas serve as temporary benchmarks that guide how the project scales in a given timeframe. Managing these quotas effectively allows for strategic growth and momentum.

## Key Takeaways
- Never let perceived limits stop your progress.
- Use constraints, like weekly quotas, as benchmarks for focused growth.
- Focus on continuous action rather than waiting for perfect conditions.
- Embrace the mindset of pushing boundaries to achieve exponential growth.
