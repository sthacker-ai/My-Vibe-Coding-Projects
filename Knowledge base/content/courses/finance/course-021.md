## Core Concepts
### Passive Income
Passive income refers to earnings generated with minimal ongoing effort or active involvement from the individual after an initial setup. Unlike active income (like a salary), passive income relies on systems, assets, or content that generate revenue continuously. The goal of this strategy is to transition from being a laborer of time to an owner of a system that generates financial returns, emphasizing leverage over direct labor.

### Digital Arbitrage
Digital arbitrage is the practice of exploiting price differences or opportunities across different digital platforms or markets to achieve a profit. In the context of this course, it involves identifying valuable digital content (like a YouTube video) and using automated systems to extract or re-package that content for monetization, effectively capitalizing on the value of someone else's work without performing the original labor.

### Content Leverage
Content leverage is the strategy of using existing, high-value content as the foundation for income generation, rather than creating new content from scratch. This involves identifying content that already has an audience and monetizing it through automation. This concept leverages the audience engagement and distribution already established by the original creator, allowing the user to focus only on the technical setup rather than the creation process.
