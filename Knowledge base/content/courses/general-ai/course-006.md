## Core Concepts

### General AI (AGI)
General Artificial Intelligence refers to the theory and development of machines that can understand, learn, and apply knowledge to solve complex problems across diverse domains, mirroring the cognitive flexibility of a human. Unlike Narrow AI, which is specialized for a single task (e.g., playing chess or facial recognition), AGI aims for flexible, human-like reasoning. The pursuit of AGI is not just a technical goal; it is a philosophical endeavor to understand the nature of intelligence itself and how it can be replicated, which fundamentally changes how we define intelligence, consciousness, and problem-solving.

## Common Pitfalls / What to Watch Out For
Beginners often fall into the trap of treating AI outputs as infallible truth. A major pitfall is the failure to engage in critical verification; users often accept complex answers without understanding the reasoning pathway, leading to the propagation of misinformation or flawed logic (hallucinations). Another common mistake is ignoring the potential biases embedded within the training data of the AI, which can lead to unfair or contextually inappropriate results. Furthermore, users must be cautious about over-reliance; treating the AI as a replacement for critical thought rather than a sophisticated tool risks eroding essential human intellectual skills. Always maintain the principle that the human remains the final authority and the ultimate validator of the information.
